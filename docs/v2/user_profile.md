# Wyrypa User Profile (Extracted from 100km Legacy Data)

This document contains the physiological and tactical profile extracted from the successful 100km / 24h extreme ultra-trekking challenge.

## 1. Pacing & Capabilities
- **Baseline Pace (Flat/Mild Terrain):** ~13:45 to 14:15 min/km.
- **Steep Ascents Penalty:** Pace drops to ~16:00 min/km on steep climbs (e.g., +500m over 5km).
- **Descent / Flat Recovery:** Capable of maintaining ~13:40 min/km after long downhills.
- **Fatigue Curve:** Remarkable stamina. The pace only degrades by roughly +30 to +45 seconds/km after 60km. In the final 20km, pace holds steady at ~14:20 min/km despite fatigue.

## 2. Nutrition Strategy
- **Hydration Base:** "Hyper-Mix" (High-calorie isotonic/carbo mix) is the primary fuel source. 1 flask is consumed approximately every 4-5 hours (or every 20-25km).
- **Solid Food:** Consumed early in the race (e.g., Sandwich at km 15, Banana at km 35).
- **Transition to Liquids:** By km 85, solid food is completely stopped. The stomach only accepts Energy Gels and liquids from this point to the finish line.
- **Sodium Management:** 2x SaltStick capsules are taken every 4-5 hours to prevent cramps, with "Max Sól" protocols engaged during high-heat periods.
- **Quick Energy / Sugar:** Dextro Energy cubes are used strategically between major climbs (e.g., km 20, km 75).

## 3. Crisis Management & Tactical Triggers
- **Night Crisis (circa km 45, 05:20 AM):** Sleep deprivation hits hard. Protocol: Drink Red Bull #1 + Glucose 1WW.
- **Heat Crisis (circa km 80, 14:11 PM):** If racing during summer daylight. Protocol: Red Bull #2 + Max Salt + Liquid only.
- **Footwear/Hygiene (The "Przepak"):** A mandatory 30-minute drop-bag break occurs around 60% of the distance (km 60). Actions: Change shoes, eat hot soup, access drop bag.
- **Micro-breaks:** 5-minute stops are utilized for critical maintenance (e.g., changing socks at km 25) rather than pure rest.

## 4. Overall Assessment
This profile indicates a highly experienced ultra-trekker with a very strong cardiovascular base and a resilient stomach that only shuts down very late in the race (>85km). The strategy relies heavily on liquid calories (Hyper-Mix) and aggressive caffeine/glucose injections during known circadian lows (dawn) and late-stage heat.
